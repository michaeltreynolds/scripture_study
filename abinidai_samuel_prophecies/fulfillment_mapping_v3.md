# V3 Comprehensive Fulfillment Mapping

A **left-join** of all 65 prophecies (from `prophecies_v2.md`) against the full Nephite history after each prophecy's location. Built from careful reading of every relevant narrative chapter, supplemented by keyword search recon across 839 candidate matches.

**Method**: Chapter-by-chapter reading of Mosiah 19–24, Alma 14, Alma 25, Helaman 11, 3 Nephi 1, 3 Nephi 8–10, 3 Nephi 23, 4 Nephi 1, Mormon 1–6, Moroni 9, supported by keyword search across the full text.

**Confidence Scale**:
- 🟢 **High (0.85–1.0)** — Explicit textual match, often with direct cross-reference
- 🟡 **Medium (0.50–0.84)** — Strong thematic match, consistent language or events
- 🔴 **Low (0.20–0.49)** — Possible but indirect; requires interpretive lens

## Editorial Notes

### 1. Mormon's Retrospective Framing
Mormon 1:19 explicitly states: "even **unto the fulfilling of all the words of Abinadi, and also Samuel the Lamanite**." Mormon, writing decades later, views his own era as the culmination of the prophetic arc. He is not saying every prophecy was fulfilled in one moment, but that as he surveyed the full history of the Nephites — from the bondage of Limhi's people to the slippery treasures of his own day — he could check off every warning.

### 2. Cumulative Fulfillment
Many prophecies are fulfilled *repeatedly* across Nephite history. Famine comes in Helaman 11, then again in the Mormon-era wars. Prophets are cast out in 3 Nephi 7, then again in 4 Nephi. The devil's power grows in 4 Nephi 1:28 and reaches its peak in Mormon 1:19. The mapping above captures the *primary* fulfillment instances, but the pattern is cyclical.

### 3. The Three Prophecies with Low Confidence
- **A12** (hail, east wind, insects) — No explicit narrative. Possibly fulfilled "off-text" in the portions Mormon abridged.
- **A13** (great pestilence) — The word "pestilence" appears in Helaman but not specifically for Noah's people.
- **A21** (all manner of diseases) — No narrative about diseases among the priests' descendants specifically.

These three share a common theme: they concern **agricultural/biological afflictions** that may have occurred but were not recorded in the abridged history. Mormon repeatedly notes he cannot write "a hundredth part" of the record.

### 4. The Verbatim Fulfillments
Several prophecies are fulfilled so exactly that the text uses nearly identical language:
- A5/Mosiah 21:15 — "slow to hear their cry"
- A8/Mosiah 21:3 — "smitten on the cheek…drive them as they would a dumb ass"
- A11/Mosiah 21:3 — "heavy burdens upon their backs"
- A22/Alma 25:12 — "these words were verified"
- S17/Mormon 1:18 — "became slippery"
- S26/3 Nephi 1:15,19 — "no darkness"
- S31/3 Nephi 8:18 — "seams and cracks"
- S34/3 Nephi 23:9–11 — Christ confirms Samuel's prophecy was "all fulfilled"

### 5. Christ as Validator
In 3 Nephi 23:9–13, Christ himself reviews the Nephite records and commands that Samuel's prophecy about the risen saints be recorded. This is a unique instance of the subject of a prophecy (the Messiah) validating the prophet's words — and correcting the record to ensure completeness.
