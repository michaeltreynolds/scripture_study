## Key Observations

### V2 Improvements
1. **S23 (Helaman 13:37) is now surfaced as a key connection.** "Surrounded by demons" → sorceries/witchcrafts is arguably one of the top 3 strongest fulfillments in the chapter. V1 buried this inside a compressed "lamentation" entry.
2. **A17–A18 (Mosiah 16:3, 5) are now included.** Abinadi's teaching that "the devil has power" and "the devil hath **all power**" over the unrepentant maps directly to Mormon's "the power of the evil one was wrought upon all the face of the land." These were completely omitted in V1 because V1 classified them as purely doctrinal rather than predictive — but they are clearly *both*.

### Top 3 Strongest Fulfillments
1. **Slippery treasures** (S9, S17, S20–S22) — Samuel prophesies the phenomenon across 6+ verses; Mormon describes it nearly verbatim.
2. **Surrounded by demons → sorceries/witchcrafts** (S23 + A17/A18) — Samuel prophesies demonic infestation; Abinadi prophesies devil's "all power"; Mormon describes the specific manifestations.
3. **Spirit withdrawn** (S3) — "I will withdraw my Spirit" maps directly to "the Holy Ghost did not come upon any."

### Abinadi vs. Samuel
- **Samuel's prophecies** dominate this chapter with *specific, distinctive* fulfillments — 6 entries for slippery treasures alone.
- **Abinadi's most important contribution** (newly surfaced in V2) is A17/A18 — his teaching that the devil gains "all power" over the wicked. This is the *only* direct Abinadi prophecy that maps to Mormon 1:19's sorceries/witchcrafts and power of the evil one.
- Many of Abinadi's other prophecies (bondage, burdens, famine, death by fire) were fulfilled *centuries earlier* with King Noah's people (Mosiah 19–24, Alma 25).

## Editorial Notes

### Note 1: Mormon is writing retrospectively
Mormon 1 describes events from when Mormon was 10–15 years old, but Mormon is writing the entire book as an old man who has already witnessed the final destruction of his people at Cumorah (Mormon 6). When he writes *"even unto the fulfilling of **all** the words"* (1:19), he is speaking with the full weight of hindsight. The conditions in chapter 1 are the *beginning* of the fulfillment, not the completion — but Mormon already knows how the story ends. From his editorial vantage point, the trajectory is unmistakable and every prophecy has been or will clearly be fulfilled.

### Note 2: "All the words" spans the entire Nephite history, not just Mormon's time
Mormon's claim that "all the words" of Abinadi and Samuel are fulfilled should not be read as saying that Mormon 1 alone checks every prophetic box. As an editor who has read and abridged the entire Nephite record, Mormon is surveying the *cumulative arc* from the time those prophecies were made through centuries of Nephite history up to his present — and what he knows comes next. Many prophecies were fulfilled long before Mormon's day: famines occurred in Helaman's time, Abinadi's prophecies about King Noah's people were fulfilled in Mosiah 19–24 and Alma 25, and the signs of Christ's birth and death were fulfilled in 3 Nephi. Mormon is an editor-historian checking off each prophecy against the full historical record, not claiming that the specific conditions of his youth in chapter 1 exhaust the list. The verse is saying: all the prophecies are fulfilled — not that his exact time fulfills all the prophecies.
