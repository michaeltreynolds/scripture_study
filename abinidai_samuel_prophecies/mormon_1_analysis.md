# Mormon 1 — Verse-by-Verse Analysis

What is Mormon describing in each verse? Is he describing **conditions of his time** that relate to prophecy fulfillment, or is he providing narrative setup?

---

| Verse | Text Summary | Category | Conditions Described |
|-------|-------------|----------|---------------------|
| 1:1 | Mormon introduces himself and his record | **Narrative setup** | None |
| 1:2 | Ammaron comes to 10-year-old Mormon, recognizes him as sober and observant | **Narrative setup** | None |
| 1:3 | Ammaron tells Mormon to retrieve the records at age 24 from the hill Shim | **Narrative setup** | None |
| 1:4 | Instructions: take the plates of Nephi, engrave what he observes | **Narrative setup** | None |
| 1:5 | Mormon remembers Ammaron's charge | **Narrative setup** | None |
| 1:6 | Mormon (age 11) carried to the land of Zarahemla | **Narrative setup** | None |
| 1:7 | The land covered with buildings, people numerous as sand | **Conditions of the time** | Dense population, extensive building |
| 1:8 | War begins between Nephites and Lamanites | **Conditions of the time** | War / conflict between factions |
| 1:9 | The two parties identified: Nephites and Lamanites | **Narrative clarification** | None |
| 1:10 | War begins in the borders of Zarahemla, by the waters of Sidon | **Conditions of the time** | War centered at Zarahemla |
| 1:11 | Nephites gather 30,000+; many battles; Nephites prevail and slay many | **Conditions of the time** | Massive warfare, bloodshed |
| 1:12 | Lamanites withdraw; peace for ~4 years | **Conditions of the time** | Temporary peace |
| 1:13 | Wickedness prevails; the Lord takes away his beloved disciples; miracles and healing cease | **Conditions of the time** | Pervasive wickedness; spiritual gifts withdrawn; disciples removed |
| 1:14 | No gifts from the Lord; Holy Ghost does not come upon any because of wickedness and unbelief | **Conditions of the time** | Spirit of the Lord withdrawn; unbelief |
| 1:15 | Mormon (age 15) visited by the Lord, knows the goodness of Jesus | **Personal narrative** | None (Mormon is an exception to the general wickedness) |
| 1:16 | Mormon forbidden to preach; the people had willfully rebelled; beloved disciples taken away | **Conditions of the time** | Willful rebellion; righteous removed from the land |
| 1:17 | Mormon forbidden to preach because of hardness of hearts; **the land was cursed for their sake** | **Conditions of the time** | Hardness of hearts; **cursed land** |
| 1:18 | Gadianton robbers infest the land; people **hide up treasures** in the earth; **they became slippery** because the Lord had cursed the land, so they **could not hold them nor retain them** | **Conditions of the time** | Gadianton robbers; **treasures hidden and becoming slippery**; cursed land |
| 1:19 | **Sorceries, witchcrafts, and magics**; power of the evil one upon the land; **"even unto the fulfilling of all the words of Abinadi, and also Samuel the Lamanite"** | **Conditions of the time** | Sorcery/witchcraft/magic; evil power prevails; **Mormon explicitly states this fulfills Abinadi and Samuel** |

---

## Summary of Conditions in Mormon's Time (vv. 7–19)

1. **Dense population, extensive buildings** (v. 7)
2. **War between Nephites and Lamanites** (vv. 8, 10–11)
3. **Massive bloodshed** (v. 11)
4. **Pervasive wickedness across the whole land** (v. 13)
5. **The Lord's disciples taken away / righteous removed** (vv. 13, 16)
6. **Miracles, healing, and spiritual gifts cease** (vv. 13–14)
7. **The Holy Ghost withdrawn** (v. 14)
8. **Willful rebellion against God** (v. 16)
9. **Hardness of hearts** (v. 17)
10. **The land cursed** (v. 17)
11. **Gadianton robbers infest the land** (v. 18)
12. **Treasures hidden in the earth become slippery — cannot be held or retained** (v. 18)
13. **Sorceries, witchcrafts, and magics** (v. 19)
14. **The power of the evil one prevails upon the whole land** (v. 19)
